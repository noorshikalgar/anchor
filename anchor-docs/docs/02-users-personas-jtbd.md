# 02 — Users, Personas & Jobs-To-Be-Done

## Primary persona: "The Overloaded Improver"

- Late 20s, urban India, lives with parents/extended family or in a shared
  household where routine isn't fully under their own control.
- Working professional, often with unpredictable hours (client calls, deadlines,
  on-call work).
- Has a long mental list of self-improvement goals (sleep, diet, gym, reading,
  learning to code, grooming/hair care) and genuinely wants to act on all of them.
- Has tried habit apps or a Notion template before; it worked for a week, then
  a busy patch (relatives visiting, a work crunch, a late-night show binge) broke
  the routine and they never went back.
- Is not lazy or undisciplined — the actual failure mode is **planning failure**,
  not motivation failure. They don't know what to prioritize, and the plan they
  had couldn't flex when life didn't cooperate.
- Comfortable with technology, wants something that respects their time (fast to
  log, not a 20-minute setup ritual) and doesn't infantilize them with cartoon
  mascots or guilt-trip notifications.

## Secondary persona (design for, don't over-index on): "The Fresh Starter"

- Anyone starting a self-improvement push from zero — a student, a new grad, a
  person post-breakup or post-illness rebuilding a routine. Same core need
  (prioritization + disruption-resilience), lower initial habit count.

## Jobs-to-be-done

Framed as "when ... I want to ... so I can ..."

1. **When** I have many things I want to fix in my life, **I want to** know which
   one to start with, **so I can** avoid spreading myself too thin and quitting
   everything.
2. **When** I've stabilized one habit, **I want to** be told when and how to add
   the next one, **so I can** keep progressing without a rigid rulebook.
3. **When** something disrupts my day (guests, extended work, a late night),
   **I want to** have a pre-defined "smaller version" of my habits, **so I can**
   stay in the game instead of feeling like I've failed and giving up entirely.
4. **When** I look back at a week, **I want to** see what actually happened
   (not just streaks), **so I can** understand my own patterns (e.g. "I always
   skip gym on days my in-laws visit").
5. **When** a new week starts, **I want** a proposed plan generated for me,
   **so I can** just react/adjust instead of re-deciding my whole life from
   scratch every Sunday night.
6. **When** I'm deciding what to do at a specific time of day (e.g. after
   dinner), **I want** the app to suggest the next best action from my current
   focus habits, **so I can** avoid decision fatigue and default-scrolling
   instead.

## Explicit anti-goals from the user's perspective

- "Don't make me feel guilty when life happens — I live with family, I don't
  fully control my schedule."
- "Don't turn my self-improvement into a game with pets and XP — I'm an adult,
  I want a system, not a toy."
- "Don't ask me to track 15 things — help me *not* do that."
